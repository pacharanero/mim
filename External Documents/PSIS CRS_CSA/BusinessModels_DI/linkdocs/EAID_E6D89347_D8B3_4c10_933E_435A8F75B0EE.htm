2.1    DI Report for single requested procedure but multiple performed procedures

Ken Golden has severe left-side loin pain and haematuria on urine testing. His GP, Natalie Atkinson refers him for an urgent IVU for a presumed diagnosis of renal colic. The IVU is supervised by the radiologist, Dr. Carlene Purves who observes no excretion from the left kidney after 45 mins. She therefore transfers the patient to the ultrasound dept. The request for this new procedure is appended to the original request. The ultrasound shows an obstructive left kidney containing debris and suggesting infection.

Dr. Purves proceeds to review the images acquired. Dr. Purves reports on the procedures and verifies the completed reports. As both procedures are portions of the same request and there are no outstanding procedures to be performed, reported or verified, the RIS now assumes the request to be fulfilled and proceeds to formulate the DI Report communication.

Once completed the RIS sends out the DI report communication according to the instructions received in the request and the urgency status.

The DI Report is received by the GP, Natalie Atkinson with a status of urgent. The DI Report is also sent to PSIS.

    1. Addendum to DI Report

As a result of both procedures carried out on Ken Golden, Dr. Carlene Purves speaks to the urology consultant Mr. Leahy about referring Ken Golden. Following the conversation Dr. Purves decides to refer Ken Golden to the urology department for a nephrostomy placement. Dr Purves adds an addendum to the previous version of the report to this effect, verifies it and re-issues the report. Version 2 of the DI report is sent out to the same recipients as the previous version and replaces the previous version of the report on PSIS.

 

2.3    DI Report message containing a DI Summary Report with multiple DI Procedure Reports

 
Sidney Morrison goes to to see his GP, Dr. Natalie Atkinson.

He tells Dr. Atkinson that for several days now he hasn’t been able to move his left elbow and has pain in his wrist. Additionally he has noticed some bruising on his left shoulder. Dr. Gillespie asks his if he has had a fall. Sidney can’t remember having done so.

On examination the doctor finds that Sidney’s shoulder movement is badly impaired as is his elbow and wrist.

A couple of x-ray examinations are in order, so Dr. Atkinson fills out a request for x- ray examination on Sidney’s left upper arm to include shoulder and elbow.

In the radiology department at Hebburn hospital, Sidney is taken into an x-ray room and his left shoulder and elbow procedures are done separately by the radiographer, using the PACS system. When the radiographer views the images she sees that Sidney has a fracture of the humeral head and another of the radial head in his elbow. She performs a few extra views of each joint and then asks Sidney to sit outside whilst she shows the films to the Radiologist.

Dr Melisa Carr, the Radiologist, reports the procedures, separately as she sees them on the PACS workstation.

Whilst these injuries are not uncommon, they are rarely found together on the same patient from the same incident so she decides to do a ‘Summary’ report to suggest to the GP that Sidney may have had several falls recently and that she should probably be referred to the Care of the Elderly team for further investigation if A&E do not do so.

Once completed and verified the RIS sends out the DI report communication according to the instructions received in the request and the urgency status.

The DI Report is received by the GP, Natalie Atkinson. The DI Report is also sent to PSIS and is available to view by the A&E Department.
