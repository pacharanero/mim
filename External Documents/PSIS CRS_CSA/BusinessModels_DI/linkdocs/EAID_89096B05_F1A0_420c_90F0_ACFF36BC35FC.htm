Glossary of Terms:

Term
Acronym
Definition
Care Record System
CRS
A local implementation of a care record system.
Addendum
An addendum is used to report information that is added as an addendum after the original dictation and is sent as a separate labeled section of the report.
Partial Report
This is a DI Report which is final and verified but is likely to have additional information added to it e.g. a report for an additional procedure/request.
Interim Report
This is a DI Report that is given to the patient's clinician, either by verbal or handwritten means, at the time (or shortly after) the DI Procedure has been completed. It is given on the understanding that a more concise (and possibly different) report will be added to the patient record once the reporter has had time to review more fully the images acquired during the procedure, any other pertinent materials and/or consulted with colleagues.
If an Interim Report is given electronically it should be considered to be an unverified report.
Summary Report
This is a part of a report which is common to/refers to all of the procedures reported on in the report.
Replacement Report
A report intended to completely replace an existing report where such previous report contains information subsequently shown to be wrong or misleading.  Following issuing of a replacement report, the previous report would only be available for auditing purposes or on specific request, so as to reduce the risk of accidental clinical use.

  1. Purpose

    The purpose of this document is to provide the necessary background/information required to determine the scope of the analysis products that are to be created for the Diagnostic Imaging, 2008-B domain.

  • Statement of domain business aims and objectives, including the business needs, problems to be solved and an outline of the benefits to be delivered

  • Identification of which analysis artefacts are required for this project

  • Scope for each of the analysis artefacts identified

  • Assumptions to be made in producing the artefacts

  • Control arrangements in terms of ownership and custodianship

    1. Introduction

      1. Domain/ Business Area Description

        Currently the Picture Archiving and Communication System (PACS) facilitates the storage of patient images such as x-rays, scans and ultrasounds.

        DI encounters take place between Healthcare Professionals from a Diagnostic Imaging department and the patient, following a request/order from a registered Primary or Secondary Care medical practitioner (Placer/DI Order Requester) to a Diagnostic Imaging department (Filler/DI Order Requester).

        Requests require the performing of a requested procedure (or procedures if more than one procedure has been grouped under the same SNOMED CT procedure code), and each procedure may consist of one or more procedure steps resulting in one or more Diagnostic Images being captured.

        The images are reviewed by the performing specialist, who records the completed Imaging Procedure details on the Radiology Information System (RIS). The requested procedures and performed procedures should be noted in the report. The performed procedures may differ from the procedures originally requested if an alternative procedure was carried out. The images are reviewed, usually by a Radiologist(s), who creates a DI Report on the images.

        Figure 1 (below) outlines the status of request and report messaging, as supported by currently deployed order comms, PAS, RIS, etc. applications.  Where electronically capable, HL7 v2.x messages support the request and report business process model between a requester, fulfiller and information recipients.

        Figure 1 - Current Requesting and Reporting

        This model also supports the Choose and Book indirectly bookable service for Diagnostic Imaging services since an administrative process is required to progress appointments within the Radiology domain.

      2. Domain Objectives & Predicted Benefits


    Two of the objectives of DI Domain are:

    • Integrate radiology information with patient records

    • Facilitate access to images associated with DI Reports


    The objective and predicted benefit of this phase of Diagnostic Imaging is to make Diagnostic Imaging Reports from Primary and Secondary care, available outside the Radiology domain e.g. in an Accident and Emergency setting. DI Reports from Primary and Secondary care would be available as CDA (Clinical Document Architecture) documents stored in PSIS and would be available to view on CSA, GP Systems, HealthSpace and other clinical systems as part of a patient's Summary Care Record.  These systems can retrieve the DI Report document type using the PSIS Query message. The DI Report based on the performed procedure(s) and containing the clinical conclusions, is sent to the requesting clinician, Personal Spine Information Service (PSIS) and “copy reports” to any other specified recipients. The images would be accessible via the DI report.


    There are additional standards-based benefits of this work including:

    • The new DI Report messages are defined to the latest, most current messaging standards utilised by NHS CFH, HL7 v3.0 CDA messages
    • Support for SNOMED coding standards

    1. Domain Analysis Requirements

    • Completion, verification and dispatch of a final report for a specific request from radiology to the nominated recipients including PSIS.

    • Updating a retrieved or local copy of the report and replacing the existing report on PSIS:

      • Partial reports will be available on PSIS where futher procedure reports associated with the requested procedures within a DI Report are pending; this is because it is imperative to know that the patient has been irradiated. The partial DI Report can be updated and replaced to allow progression of the DI Report. If a group of procedures are carried out in a single encounter then these may be reported together e.g. head, chest and abdomen. This could result in the generation of a single DI Report message for all three procedures; the procedure report section of the DI Report would apply to all three of the requested procedures. If the procedures were reported at different times then a DI Report message could be sent to PSIS as each procedure is completed, the additional DI Report would update and replace the previous DI Report message. The replaced report is no longer available to view on PSIS. A status would be held against each procedure request in the DI Report Message indicating its status - open or completed. Once a requested procedure has been reported on PSIS, the associated procedure report should be final and verified. Where more than one procedure is being reported on within a DI Report message, a summary report may be supplied in addition to or instead of the individual procedure reports.

      • Where an addendum to a report is required to be added to the DI Report. An addendum may be sent as a “second opinion” on a final DI report and would be added as an additional part of the report. The original report should be updated and replace the version on PSIS and an updated report should be sent to other recipients of the original report.

      • Where a report is found to be in error and was never correct it should be replaced on PSIS and previous versions of the report will be hidden from view, an updated report should also be sent to other recipients.

    • Where a report has been replaced, the previous report is no longer available to view except as part of an audit trail.

    • Retrieval and display of the DI Report in systems which display clinical documents on PSIS e.g. CSA, HealthSpace etc.

    • Display of the report in GP Systems and other systems which receive a copy of the report.

    • DI Reports can be patient sealed at the document level. It may be necessary to withhold NCRS test results from a patient on HealthSpace for a configurable national standard period of time; this may be implemented through some form of clincian sealing.

    • If a patient has dissented to share their record then the DI Report should only be available to view if a system overrides dissent, the only exception to this is HealthSpace. The patient should be able to view their own DI Reports regardless of whether the patient has dissented or not.


    1. Source of Requirements

      This domain has undergone many development iterations, analysed in the Primary Care and Secondary Care contexts and with and without messaging to PSIS. Earlier versions of the domain anticipated the availability of PSIS functionality for workflow purposes (this functionality will not be available in the 2008-B release of PSIS). The respective scoping documents for these different pieces of analysis are inputs to the 2008-B analysis [ref. 5, 6, 7 and 8]. The DI domain has also featured in MiM 6.2 [ref. 3].

      Currently HL7 v2.x messages support the request and report business process model between a requester, fulfiller and information recipients. A mapping exercise was undertaken between the previous analysis featured in the MiM [ref. 3] and the HL7 v2 Observation Report. The IHE documentation [ref. 10] and the HL7 v2 manual [ref. 11] were used as inputs to this exercise. The previous analysis was found to largely support the same data set as the v2 messages.

      The number of messages processed by the PSIS/CSA system is to be expanded to provide enhanced functionality and features to end users of the systems. This builds upon a messaging set that was introduced in Release 2006-B2 and 2008-A by adding further clinical messages, one of which is the Diagnostic Imaging Report.

      The 2008-B Release Requirements Mandate [ref. 9] states a requirement for PACS data availability as part of the Summary Care Record. It also states a messaging requirement for diagnostic reports to be made available both to the originator and other individuals who may be involved with the care of the patient.

      For 2008-B the scope for Diagnostic Imaging messaging covers reporting from Primary or Secondary Care and sending a Diagnostic Imaging report to PSIS, requesting clinician or Trust OCS and other nominated parties. This message will conform to Connecting for Health CDA messaging.

  • Analysis Scope Definition

    1. Overview of Business Process

  • Health Care Professional (HCP) generates a report on the local system or updates an existing report (which includes updating an partial report, adding an addendum or replacing an incorrect report).

  • Report is verified by a verifying HCP before being sent to the original HCP that requested the procedure, PSIS and other interested parties.


    1. Impacted Users

  • Health Care Professional undertaking the procedure usually Radiographer (it is becoming increasingly commonly for a “reporting radiographer” to be present now in most trusts).

    • Health Care Professional creating, verifying and submitting the report usually Radiologist.

    • Clinicians outside imaging department viewing reports.


    1. Start/End Points of the Analysis

      Business processes, and their associated information requirements, to be covered by this domain analysis commence from the point where a procedure has been undertaken and a Health Care Professional records the completed Imaging Procedure details on the Radiology Information System (RIS) or Care Record System (CRS).

      The analysis ends when the report has been dispatched to PSIS and other recipients.

    2. Out of Scope Activities

      1. Domain Specific Processes Excluded from Scope

    It is common practice when developing designs for domains to concentrate on a specific area of the business domain, normally by exclusion of commonly understood areas of processes, functionality or information needs. The following specific areas of the domain under consideration are to be excluded from scope:

    • Internal management of the PACS and RIS systems prior to the production of a DI Report.

        1. 'Common Processes' Excluded from Scope

    Processes common to many domains such as establishing LR and RBAC may not be required to be included within the analysis for a particular domain/sub-set. These exclusions are normally applied to the BPM artefact. The following common activities are deemed to be outside the scope of the proposed analysis:

    • Establishing LRs and RBAC

    • Identifying the patient on PDS

      1. Out of Scope Requirements

    A number of requirements have been identified but are out of scope of the current project/release, these include the following:

    • Storage of images on PSIS

    • Requesting of procedures and any messaging or storage of these requests. Existing, HL7 v2.x DI requesting mechanism remains in place.

    • Workflow management including cancelling requests, rejecting requests etc.

    • Storage of data from the POC messages in the previous analysis on PSIS, this data includes pharamaceuticals administered, reason for administation and radiation dose.

    • Snding unverified procedure reports as part of a DI Report message to PSIS.

      1. Exception Conditions to be Included/Excluded from the Analysis

        An exception condition is deemed to be when the process is interrupted. E.g. non receipt of an acknowledgement or any other situation where checks are made and the checks fail. These exceptions can be reflected within several of the analysis artefacts including use cases, as alternative paths, and BPMs.
        NB The author of this document may not be aware of some of these conditions prior to analysis work commencing. These may need to be discussed with the 'Owner' of the document on an ad hoc basis, and a new version of the scoping document created.
        In general, the exception conditions to be explicitly modelled are of the 'flow failure' type - i.e. where a flow in the process can fail (such as the non-receipt of an acknowledgement message) NB. This applies only where the prospect of the failure is realistic specific cases of this type of failure are identified in Section 4.6.1 below.

        1. Explicit Exception Conditions

    The following exception conditions are to be included within the analysis model(s):

    • Non-receipt of acknowledgements

    • Processes involved in handling of invalid messages associated with the encounter/image reporting

        1. Assumed Exception Conditions

    The following exception conditions can be excluded from the analysis model(s):

    • Security failures (e.g. failure to create legitimate relationships or grant access)

    • Infrastructure failures

    • Breaching SLA requirements for DI Reporting

    • Receipt of unsuitable requests

    • DI Encounter failures (e.g. DNAs, Demographic Trace failure etc)

    1. Legacy Systems Considerations

  • DI Reports will continue to be sent from the RIS to PACS systems as DICOM SR communications or HL7 messages
  • PACS Existing System Providers (ESP) and the Independent Sector Treatment Centres (ISTC) are in scope for the generation of the Diagnostic Imaging (DI) domain messages. Compliance packs that define the integration of ESPs and ISTCs will include the DI domain as standard. The timeline for ESP and ISTC integration will vary by supplier.

    1. Implementation Considerations

  • There will be a phased approach to implementation. There are different delivery contracts and budgets per supplier.

  • A CCN will be required to be issued to suppliers.

  • System adoption of HL7 v3.0; Reporting and requesting currently utilises HL7 v2.x messages.
  • Maintaining appropriate data models to ensure appropriate mapping between HL7 v2.x request messages and HL7 v3.0 report messages;
  • Diagnostics imaging code mapping and usage (i.e. Read and SNOMED CT);
  • Image referencing and viewing from a diagnostic report;
  • Conformance to International standards

    1. Assumptions

      The following assumptions are to be made in undertaking this analysis:

      1. User Assumptions

  • The patient is present during the procedure (There are some encounters which only involve an excised part of the patient which are not modelled)

  • The patient will have been referred to DI from a Primary or Secondary Care encounter or from Accident and Emergency and so will be registered on PDS before attendance at the DI department (althought this may be using a temporary NHS number).

  • Members of the DI service department will have access to the patient's record, this may/may not be achieved using workgroups.

    1. System Assumptions

  • Diagnostic Imaging Service Provider systems and Primary Care systems will be NPfIT compliant with regards to Spine Directory Service (SDS), Access Control Framework (ACF), Legitimate Relationships Service (LRS) and Personal Demographics Service (PDS) connectivity.

    1. Communication Assumptions

  • The PSIS query will be available to systems as a mechanism to retrieve the DI Report.

  • The DI Report message will be a message compliant with CFH CDA standards and constraints.

  • The report will be mostly textual although some limited clinical coding (SNOMED CT) may be used

  • Currently it is planned to make the Study UID and the image (via a DICOM Object or Key Object Note) available in the message.

  • It is assumed the message will carry the following basic content:

    • NHS Number of the patient

    • A reference to the image

    • A reference to one or more DI Requests

    • Details of one or more reports on one or more DI Procedure(s) including details of Clinical Diagnosis Information, Numerical Results etc. The report may be made up of:

      • One Summary Report with one or more procedure reports and associated procedure(s).

      • One or more procedure reports with associated procedure(s)

      • One or more addenda

      • Recipients of the DI Report as a record of who the communication has been sent to.

  • Relationships to Other Major Business Processes

    • The following boundary business processes are referenced or interacted with from within the scope of this model:

    • RIS and PACS system processes

    1. Phasing Statement

      This document will cover Diagnostic Imaging reporting only from Primary and Secondary Care. The scope of the model referred to within this document is restricted to 2008-B Diagnostic Imaging domain as described in MiM 7.0. A full artefact set will not be produced due to the detailed analysis carried out previously.

    2. Backward Compatibility

      Any new Diagnostic Imaging system which is integrated to a national service, for example PSIS, will need to use the latest version of the domain communications. If a domain communication changes, then any existing Diagnostic Imaging system will need to update its communications to comply with the new requirements, and complete compliance testing to achieve accreditation. New systems sending communications to national services will be expected to support the most recent version of the domain communications.  
      Existing systems will be expected to support all communications which are current, or no more than one major version behind the current version. Systems receiving communications from national services will be expected to support the most recent version of all communications, and the previous version of the communications. The national services will support the most recent version, and the previous version of the communications. All systems sending or receiving communications to national services will be expected to migrate to the new version over a period of time; typically we would expect no more than two major versions to be supported at any point in time.
