  1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide the necessary background and information required to determine the scope of the business analysis, and associated design-related work products that are to be created, for Out of Hours (OOH) communications that will be sent to PSIS and other recipients (such as the patient's GP).


That information includes:

  • Descriptions of the domain

  • Extent of the analysis artefacts to be produced by definition of the start and end points for each process within scope

  • Areas of inclusion and exclusion

  • Assumptions to be made during the analysis

  • Control arrangements in terms of ownership and custodianship


  1. Introduction

    Since the original analysis was carried out on the Out of Hours domain the range of services offered by these centres/clinics has grown. The range of services that includes OOH is to be officially renamed to Urgent Care.

    The definition taken from the Department of Health publication “Direction of Travel for Urgent Care” is as follows:
    "Urgent care is the range of responses that health and care services provide to people who require - or who perceive the need for - urgent advice, care, treatment or diagnosis. People using services and carers should expect 24/7 consistent and rigorous assessment of the urgency of their care need and an appropriate and prompt response to that need"

    The key point about this is that the initial definition lies with the service user (or their carer). But, while people should expect prompt access to an assessment of the urgency of their need, this does not mean that they will necessarily get quick access to a service. Thus, very many patient contacts end (properly and safely) with advice and reassurance, or with the individual being told to make a routine appointment with their GP or other health professional.

    For simplicity this domain will still be known as Out of Hours but consideration will be given to the other services within Urgent Care.

    1. Domain Description

The domain covers the communication that will be sent to PSIS, the patient's GP (where this can be identified) and other recipients, during or following an Out of Hours encounter. This encounter may comprise any or all of the services available (telephone triage, home visit, walk-in visit, etc.).

The communication requirements for implementation of this domain will be described in the NPfIT Message Implementation Manual version 7 (MIM 7). Business processes and data models will be contained in the Business Analysis Artefacts, which will be published as part of the MIM.

As well as Out of Hours the following services will be considered:

  • Palliative Care

  • District Nursing

  • Long Term Care

There are some services that are explicitly out of scope, see §5.3.


  1. Domain Requirements

    Following a patient encounter with an OOH service (including, but not limited to, telephone advice and consultation, home visit and walk-in visit) relevant summary information related to the encounter will be sent to PSIS, with optional additional recipients including the patient's GP and other interested clinical parties.
    It is intended that generic business process models and data models will be created to satisfy the requirements of all in-scope services within the OOH domain. If it is found that a generic solution is not suitable a revision of the scoping document may be required.
    It is understood that not all local systems will support all services and will therefore only use the solution for a limited subset of the services covered by this domain.

    The implementation of this domain will be undertaken in phase 2008B. MIM 7.0 is expected to be released in December 2007.

  • Scoping Requirements

    1. Overview of Process

    • The OOH process covers the following:

    1. Initial contact with the service and related information gathering

    2. Detailed information gathering (triage)

    3. Arrangements for the consultation (where required)

    4. Clinical consultation(s)

    5. Sending of communication to PSIS, the patient's GP and other recipients

    It should be noted that primary consideration will be given to Out of Hours processes, however the Analysis will aim to ensure that the process and its supporting artefacts will accommodate other parts of Urgent Care (see §2.1) which have not been de-scoped (see §5.3)


    The process model will be designed within the following context:

    • The process may involve one or more significant events during the course of the encounter;

    • Care may be delivered with or without the Health Care Professional (HCP) having access to their local system at that time.


    The interactions with the local system and PSIS covered by this model include:

    • Recording of relevant information relating to the encounter;

    • Sending a communication to PSIS, the patient's GP and other recipients at an appropriate stage during or following the end of the encounter;

    • Multiple communications may be sent during the entirety of an encounter.

      1. Start/End Points of the Process

        The process commences with contact by the patient or patient representative to an OOH service provider. This start point may be a transfer of care from another domain.
        The process ends with the sending of the communication to PSIS and other recipients following the end of the encounter. This end point may be a transfer of care to another domain.
        Please note: The encounter may be broken and discontinuous and communications may be sent to PSIS and other recipients at varying stages throughout.

      2. Out of Scope Activities

    Processes common to many domains (such as establishing LR and RBAC, downloading of information from PSIS, etc.) may not be required to be included within the analysis of this domain.

    The following activities are deemed to be outside the scope:

    • PDS registration

    • Electronic Referrals

    • Integration between local systems

    • Integration and overlap between other domains (other than entry points to the process from other domains or exit points from the process to other domains)


    The follow Urgent Care services included in/associated with the OOH domain will not result in a communication being sent to PSIS:

    • Mental Health Team

    • Social Services

    • Emergency Dentists

      1. Exception Conditions to be Included/Excluded

        An exception condition is deemed to be when the process is interrupted, e.g. non receipt of an acknowledgement or any other situation where checks are made and the checks fail.
        NB. The author of this document may not be aware of some of these conditions prior to work commencing on the process model.
        In general, the exception conditions to be explicitly modelled are of the 'flow failure' type - i.e. where a flow in the model can.

        1. Explicit Exception Conditions

    The following exception conditions are to be included within the process model:

    • Receipt of negative acknowledgements (error responses) from TMS.


      1. Assumed Exception Conditions

    The following exception conditions can be excluded from the process model:

    • Infrastructure failures;

    • Inability to access Spine services (e.g. PDS, SDS, PSIS);

    • Requirement to merge/de-merge patients records;

    • Unavailability of the local system.

    Systems are expected to manage and recover from such exceptions.

    1. Relationships to Other Processes

    The following boundary processes are referenced or interacted within the scope of this model:

    • Telephone transfer from NHS Direct (this is only as an entry point to the process, the transfer of information from one system to another is not within scope).


    1. Assumptions

      The following assumptions are to be made in constructing this model:

      1. User Assumptions

  • The sender and receiver(s) of the communication have a legitimate relationship with the patient.

    1. System Assumptions

  • The patient has an NHS number and is registered on PDS

  • That the local system and recipient systems will be NPfIT compliant. This includes, but is not limited to;

    • Use of SSB to authenticate users

    • Use of SDS to authenticate the system and identify the recipient system for communications

    • Use of TMS to pass the communications and receive acknowledgements

    • Use of PDS to identify the patient's GP

  • At the discretion of the system provider, the system may choose to use PSIS to retrieve clinical information. Where PSIS is accessed in this way, it is mandatory for the system to also check for the existence of a Legitimate Relationship via the LRS.

    1. Messaging Assumptions

  • Any existing messages (PDS, PSIS, LRS) will operate with no further changes required.
