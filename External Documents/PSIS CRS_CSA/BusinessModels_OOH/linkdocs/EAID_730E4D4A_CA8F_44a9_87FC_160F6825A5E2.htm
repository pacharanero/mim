  1. Telephone advice requested - inability to contact patient

    1. Aims

      To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event Report message when a planned call back is attempted but not successful.

    2. Summary

      A patient telephones the Urgent Care service asking for advice. A clinician is not available to provide immediate advice so the patient is told that the clinician will ring back. When the clinician attempts the ring back the number given rings but is not answered.

    3. Detail

1.   The patient has been seen by a consultant that day and given new medication but on reading the patient information leaflet he discovers that a condition he has should have been mentioned to the doctor. He has already taken one of the tablets and since taking it is feeling unwell.
2.   He tries to speak to his own GP but the surgery is now closed for the weekend and his call is automatically put through to the Urgent Care service.
3.   The call is answered by a call receptionist who asks his name, telephone number, address, name of own GP and brief details of his concerns.
4.   The patient gives the information and explains that he has been given medication that seems to be upsetting him.
5.   Call taker identifies call as not being immediately life threatening.
6.   There are several doctors and nurses working at the Urgent Care service but all are busy with other patients. The receptionist confirms the patient's telephone number and says that someone will ring as soon as they are free, but if he gets worse he should ring back.
7.   It is over 25 minutes before the nurse who is dealing with the telephone advice calls is ready to speak to the patient. She then tries to ring the patient but the number just rings.
8.   She then deals with the next patient before trying to ring the patient again. The telephone number just rings again.
9.   Two more attempts are made to contact the patient
10. 85 minutes have elapsed since the patient initially rung.
11. A clinical decision is made either to close the call or to attempt a face to face contact by visiting the address.
12. The nurse speaks to the doctor who reviews the clinical information available and decides to close the call.

  1. Information to be recorded

    Demographic information from patient to allow match with PDS along with:-

      Current telephone number and other contact numbers
      Current address as well as normal address
      Date of Birth
      Own GP's name or surgery's name
Current symptoms and brief background information of presenting complaint, this would include name of drug prescribed.
Time of call reception.
Prioritisation of the call urgency on reception of the call.
Name of person giving initial priority.
Times of each failed contact attempt along with name of person attempting contact.
Name of clinician who makes clinical decision not to pursue call.


  1. Simple Telephone Advice

    1. Aims

      To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event Report Message when simple telephone advice is given to a patient. The advice includes that the patient should attend the Urgent Care Centre.

    2. Summary

      A mother telephones the Urgent Care service asking for advice for her 3 year old child. A clinician speaks to the mother and decides that her child's symptoms warrant a face to face consultation that cannot wait until the GP's surgery is next open. An appointment is then made for the child to attend the Urgent Care Centre that night.

    3. Detail

1.   The child has been unwell most of the day with what seems to be an upper respiratory tract infection. The mother has been using simple remedies and Paracetamol but the child is now screaming with ear pain and has a high temperature.
2.   The mother rings the own GP's surgery but it is closed and the call is diverted to the Urgent Care service.
3.   The call is answered by a call receptionist who asks the patients name, callers name and relationship, telephone number, address, name of own GP and brief details of the problem.
4.   The mother gives the information and explains that she thinks her child has an ear infection.
5.   Call taker identifies the call as not being immediately life threatening.
6.   There are several doctors and nurses working at the Urgent Care service but all are busy with other patients. The receptionist confirms the patient's telephone number and says that someone will ring as soon as they are free, but if the child gets worse the mother should ring back.
7.   The first nurse who is free studies the list of patients waiting for call backs and decides that this patient should be telephoned next.
8.   The nurse telephones the number given and confirms with the mother she is talking to the right person.
9.   The nurse takes a clinical history from the mother and decides that the child needs to be seen that night for examination. (Note - this may be achieved with computer aided decision support software)
10. The nurse decides that the child's condition would not deteriorate if moved and there are no extraordinary social circumstances preventing travel.  An appointment is then made for the child at the Urgent Care Centre. (Note - Transport may be arranged in some cases)
11. The Urgent Care Centre the patient is attending is alerted and the call details are passed.

  1. Information to be recorded

    Demographic information from mother to allow patient match with PDS along with:-

      Current telephone number and other contact numbers
      Callers name and relationship to patient
      Current address as well as normal address
      Date of Birth
      Own GP's name or surgery's name
Current symptoms and brief background information of presenting complaint.
Time of call reception.
Prioritisation of the call urgency on reception of the call.
Name of person giving initial priority.
Time of telephone advice.
Name of clinician giving advice.
History - Fever for 24 hours, severe ear pain, fever not responding to Paracetamol.
Decision to ask to attend for consultation.
Time and place of proposed appointment.

  1. Out of Hours Centre Consultation

    1. Aims

      To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event Report Message when the patient from the Simple Telephone Advice scenario attends and is seen and treated.

    2. Summary

      A mother who has already spoken to a nurse at the Urgent Care Centre brings her child to the centre who is suffering from earache. The child is seen by the clinician who diagnoses an ear infection and supplies a full course of antibiotics from stock, as all local pharmacies are closed.

    3. Detail

1.   The mother arrives at the Urgent Care Centre with her child. The appointment has been pre arranged by the nurse the mother had spoken to on the telephone.
2.   The receptionist books the child in and they wait in the waiting room.
3.   The first clinician who is free calls the child in. The clinician has full access to the previous advice given by the nurse.
4.   The clinician confirms the history and examines the child. On further questioning he finds that the child has had a previous febrile convulsion. On examination he finds that the child is pyrexial and has an Otitis Media.
5.   The clinician decides that it is appropriate to start antibiotics and that these should be started immediately. He confirms that there are no allergies to drugs and the child is on no other medication.
6.   He issues Amoxicillin syrup from stock held at the Urgent Care Centre as no local chemist is open.
7.   He asks the mother to sign the back of the FP10 form to confirm the prescription exemption status of the child.
8.   He advises the mother to continue with regular Paracetamol, as per the directions on the bottle and gives further advice on temperature control. He tells the mother to contact the child's own GP if the symptoms are not resolving within a couple of days and to call back if the child further deteriorates.

  1. Information to be recorded

    Time patient arrived at out-of-hours centre.
    Name of clinician who sees patient.
    Time consultation started.
    Additional history - previous febrile convulsion
    Examination findings - temperature of 39 degrees Celsius. Red bulging tympanic membrane. Diagnosis of Otitis Media.
    Amoxicillin syrup 125mg / 5ml three times daily, 100ml issued from stock.
    Advice to continue Paracetamol suspension and temperature control advice.
    Advised to see own GP if not resolving and to call back if any further deterioration.


  • Complex Telephone advice

    1. Aims

      To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event message when a patient calls for advice and is provided with a prescription and/or further care is arranged.

    2. Summary

      A terminally ill patient is suffering from severe constipation due to the opiate medication being taken. The carer speaks to the nurse at the Urgent Care Centre who after consulting a doctor decides that an enema is the best treatment. The doctor issues an enema from stock and prescribes further oral laxatives.  He arranges for the district nurse to visit the patient and give the enema.

    3. Detail

    • 1.   The patient is bedbound and dying from terminal lung cancer with painful bony secondaries.  The pain is being controlled with high doses of long acting oral opiates. He has not opened his bowels for the last 5 days and is now suffering with abdominal pain and distension. He wants to wait until the GP's surgery opens on Monday morning but his carer decides to ring the urgent care service for advice.
    2.   The call is answered by a call receptionist who asks the patients name, callers name and relationship, telephone number, address, name of own GP and brief details of the problem.
    3.   The carer gives the information and explains that the patient is terminally ill but in great pain from constipation.
    4.   Call taker identifies the call as not being immediately life threatening.
    5.   There are several doctors and nurses working at the urgent care service but all are busy with other patients. The receptionist confirms the patient's telephone number and says that someone will ring as soon as they are free, but if the patient gets worse the carer should ring back.
    6.   The first nurse who is free studies the list of patients waiting for call backs and decides that this patient should be telephoned next.
    7.   The nurse telephones the number given and confirms with the carer she is talking to the right person.
    8.   The nurse takes the clinical history from the carer and establishes that the patient is on high doses of slow release oral opiates but has not been given any laxatives. The patient last had his bowels open over 5 days ago and is now in considerable discomfort from constipation.
    9.   The nurse decides to speak to a doctor for further advice on management.
    10. The nurse passes the call to the doctor for further advice.
    11. The doctor when free rings the number given and confirms with the carer he is talking to the right person.
    12. The history is confirmed and he decides that treatment with an enema and starting oral laxatives would be appropriate. The doctor issues a prescription for the laxatives and an enema is supplied from stock.
    13. The call is passed to the district nursing team to administer the enema.


    1. Information to be recorded

      Demographic information from carer to allow patient match with PDS along with:-

          Current telephone number and other contact numbers
          Callers name and relationship to patient
          Current address as well as normal address
          Date of Birth
          Own GP's name or surgery's name
    Current symptoms and brief background information of presenting complaint.
    Time of call reception.
    Prioritisation of the call urgency on reception of the call.
    Name of person giving initial priority.
    Time of telephone advice.
    Name of clinician giving advice.
    History - Terminally ill with lung cancer. On opiates for pain. Constipation with abdominal pain.
    Decision to pass call to doctor.
    Time of second telephone contact.
    Name of clinician giving further advice.
    sodium citrate microenema (5ml once)
    Lactulose (15ml tds)
    Call passed to community  nursing team


    1. OOH Initiated Consultation

      1. Aims

        To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event message when the OOH provider is asked to see a patient by a third party (Abnormal lab test etc…)

      2. Summary

        It is early Friday evening and the laboratory rings a GP's surgery with a highly abnormal blood test result. The GP's surgery is now closed for the night and the call is automatically transferred to the urgent care service. The result is such that the patient requires urgent treatment.

      3. Details

    1.   The patient had been to see his GP that day because of some abnormal bruising on his body.
    2.   His own GP had examined him and organised clotting studies and a full blood count. The doctor had asked the patient to get the blood test done that afternoon.
    3.   The results of the full blood count showed a dangerously low platelet count so the results were rung through to the GP's surgery.
    4.   The GP's surgery had closed for the weekend and the call was automatically transferred to the urgent care service.
    5.   The call is answered by a call receptionist who asks the patients name, address and telephone number, and the callers name and contact details along with the name of the patients own GP and brief details of the problem.
    6.   The call is passed to a doctor who telephones the patient.
    7.   He confirms he is talking to the right person, explains that the blood test is abnormal and takes further clinical history. He discovers that the patient is now also getting nose bleeds and decides that immediate referral for admission to the local hospital is needed.
    8.   The doctor speaks to the consultant haematologist and admission through the A&E department is arranged for that night.
    9.   Transport is arranged to get the patient to hospital.

    1. Information to be recorded

      Demographic information from lab technician to allow patient match with PDS along with:-

          Current telephone number and other contact numbers
          Callers name
          Own GP's name or surgery's name
    Current problem, blood test values and brief background.
    Time of call reception.
    Name of clinician who telephoned patient
    Time of call
    History obtained - Nose bleeds and further bruising
    Decision to admit - name of hospital and consultant accepting patient.
    Transport arranged.


    1. OOH initiated referral to emergency ambulance

      1. Aims

        To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event message when the OOH provider decides that an emergency ambulance is the most appropriate response

      2. Summary

        Patient's relative rings the urgent care service on behalf of collapsed patient who was complaining of chest pain and shortness of breath prior to their collapse.  The call taker (non clinically qualified but trained) decides that this is a life threatening emergency and an ambulance is the most appropriate response.

      3. Details

    1.   The patient has no previous history of heart problems but suddenly develops chest pain and shortness of breath.
    2.   The relative decides to ring the doctor's surgery for advice but the surgery is closed and the call is transferred automatically to the urgent care provider.
    3.   The call is answered by a call receptionist who asks the patients name, callers name and relationship, telephone number, address, name of own GP and brief details of the problem.
    4.   Using decision support (either computer based or paper) the call taker identifies the case as being immediately life threatening.
    5.   The ambulance service is contacted and the details passed across to them (ideally electronically).
    6.   The call is closed as being passed to ambulance service.

    1. Information to be recorded

      Demographic information relative to allow patient match with PDS along with:-

          Current telephone number
          Callers name
          Own GP's name or surgery's name
    Current problem
    Identified as being immediately life threatening
    Name of call taker making decision
    Time of call reception.
    Time call passed to ambulance service

    1. Death occurring during out-of-hours period

      1. Aims

        To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event message when a patient dies

      2. Summary

        Patient's relative rings the urgent care service on behalf of patient who has just died following long illness.

      3. Details

    1.   The patient has been unwell with terminal cancer and has now died
    2.   The relative rings the urgent care service as the relative believes the patient has now died.
    3.   The call is answered by a call receptionist who asks the patients name, callers name and relationship, telephone number, address, name of own GP and brief details of the problem.
    4.   Call taker identifies the call as not being immediately life threatening and is not an unexpected sudden death.
    5.   Call is passed to the clinician who sees the patient at home and confirms that the patient has died
    6.   Clinician confirms that it is an expected death.
    7.   Clinician confirms that the patient's own GP is likely to be able to issue a certificate of medical cause of death.
    8.   Clinician decides that the coroner does not need to be involved.
    9.   Clinician advises relatives to call undertakers.

    1. Information to be recorded

      Demographic information relative to allow patient match with PDS along with:-

          Current telephone number
          Callers name
          Own GP's name or surgery's name
    Current problem
    Time of call reception.
    Name of clinician visiting patient
    Time patient seen
    Time of death
    Death expected (not sudden and not suspicious)
    GP likely to issue Medical Cause of Death Certificate

    1. Failure to attend booked Urgent Care Centre consultation

      1. Aims

        To provide an example of the information required in a Care Event Report Message when the patient from the Simple Telephone Advice scenario fails to turn up at the urgent care centre

      2. Summary

        A mother who has already spoken to a nurse at the urgent care centre fails to bring her child to the centre who is suffering from earache.

      3. Detail

    1.   The child is expected at the urgent care centre at a certain time but four hours later the child has still not attended
    2.   The receptionist rings the home telephone number but there is no answer.
    3.   Following the local protocol the receptionist rings the local accident and emergency department and finds out that the patient has attended there.
    4.   The call is closed.
    5.   An explanatory message is added to the call of did not attend - attended local Emergency Department


    1. Information to be recorded

    Time patient contacted
    Time call closed
    Reason - attended ED
