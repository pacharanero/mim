Scenarios for MH&SM Domain Communications
June 2006 2008B v1.1 2 Draft


1.0          Introduction

This document outlines the Communications used within the Mental Health (MH&SM) Domain and describes example scenarios to support their use.

The areas within the domain are collectively described using the acronym MH&SM and include: Mental Health Adults, Older Persons & MH Forensic, CAMHS (Child & Adolescent Mental Health Services) and Substance Misuse (Addictions) Services. It also describes the cases in which MH&SM information collected outside of MH&SM Specialist Services can be sent to PSIS.

2.0          Domain Communications

The communications identified for the MH&SM Domain are as follows:
  • MHA Assessment (where completed outside of MH&SM-SS)
  • MHA Assessment Update (where MHA '83 activities continue outside of Specialist Services)
  • MH&SM-SS Entry
  • MH&SM-SS Update
  • MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary -
    • Including the following types:
      • CPA Summary
      • MOC Summary
      • Unspecified Summary
  • MH&SM-SS Exit

  1. 3.0          MHA Assessment Outside Specialist Services

The trigger for generating a MHA Assessment communication is that a Mental Health Act 1983 assessment has been completed outside of MH&SM-Specialist Services. The MHA Assessment is the formal process through which individuals are assessed for detention in hospital under the Act.

N.B. Where an MHA Assessment takes place during an MH&SM-SS care spell, then, depending on the point at which the assessment takes place it will be recorded using either an MH&SM-SS Entry or MH&SM- SS Update communication.

MHA Assessments take the form of recommendations following an assessment of the patient's mental well-being. These may be carried out in a variety of ways as described below:
  • by one doctor with the approval of the patient's Nearest Relative (for detention under Section 4 of the Act);
  • by two doctors (one of which should be MHA approved - under section 12); and
  • by an Approved Social Worker (ASW) for detention under section 2 or section 3 of the Act.

A doctor may also detain an in-patient under Section 5.2 of the Act and a Nurse may detain an in-patient under Section 5.4 of the Act.

Once the relevant HSCP (Health and Social Care Professional) records the occurrence of these activities on a local PSIS (Personal Spine Information Service) compliant system, it will trigger the sending of an MHA Assessment communication to PSIS.

It should be noted that sometimes real-life activities take place at a fast pace and not necessarily in a defined order. The first entry regarding a care episode might therefore be an MH&SM-SS Entry (see below) created at admission to the ward. In this instance the MHA '83 Assessment would be recorded subsequently by, for example the ASW (Approved Social Worker) using an MH&SM-SS Update communication. There is no co-dependency between the two types of communications (MHA Assessment and MH&SM-SS Entry).

As stated above, where a MHA Assessment takes place during a MH&SM-SS care spell, it will be recorded using either an MH&SM-SS Entry or MH&SM-SS Update communication

Scenario 1
Mr Ahmed Khan (Aged 79) was assessed under the Mental Health Act 1983 at his home by Dr Joan Strong Consultant Psychiatrist and his GP.

Mr Khan's wife, Ashum Begum was present. On completion of the assessment Mr Khan is deemed as not detainable and although it would be appropriate to admit him to the local inpatient unit for a thorough assessment, he refused this. He is therefore referred to the local Community Mental Health Team (CMHT) for Older Adults for assessment.

Joan returns to her office and enters the information gathered during the MHA assessment onto her local MH Partnership Trust System which generates an MHA Assessment message for publication to PSIS
Scenario 2
Helen Jones (aged 27) was admitted into the intensive care unit of Greenwood Hospital at 03:00 last night having taken a large but as yet undetermined dose of Paracetamol. Helen was admitted unconscious and has been admitted to a ward to monitor her condition and in particular her liver function. There are concerns for Helen's mental well being as a result of this action because she has been admitted previously in similar circumstances. Her physical care is the main priority at this time so she is being cared for in a general hospital rather than within an MH&SM Specialist Service. There are concerns however that when Helen regains consciousness she may seek to discharge herself. Because of this, the Consultant, Dr Andrews, records his concern on her local healthcare record.

Helen regains consciousness three hours later and decides she wants to go home. She removes her intravenous infusion, gets out of bed and attempts to get dressed. In light of her past history and the consultant's concerns the Senior House Officer (SHO) - Dr Geoff Small, decides that she needs to be detained and does so under the provisions of Section 5.2 of the Mental Health Act 1983 until the morning when the Approved Social Worker (ASW) will be available. Dr Small also makes a recommendation at that time that Helen should be detained under Section 2 of the Act. The next morning Joan Harper (ASW) and Dr Sandra Westwood, a MHA Section 12 approved doctor, carry out a MHA '83 assessment and decide to recommend Helen is detained under Section 2 MHA '83.

Following the completion of the relevant forms, they are presented to the person authorised to receive them in the Hospital for checking and approval. On approval, Dr Small (SHO) records the detention on Helen's record using the local system which in turn generates an MHA Assessment communication for transmission to PSIS.

When Helen has recovered sufficiently she will be seen by a Psychiatric liaison nurse and may at that time be admitted into care by a MH&SM-Specialist Service.

  1. 4.0          MHA Assessment Update Outside Specialist Services

This communication is triggered by a local system updating any of the information items contained within an already recorded MHA Assessment communication as follows:
  • When care is continuing outside of an MH&SM-SS (e.g. where a patient is receiving care for their physical needs within a non- MH&SM service)
  • Where the patient is currently detained in hospital under the MHA '83
  • To support the updating of the legal status of the patient under MHA '83 or information relevant to that assessment

Scenario
After a few days of care on Hatton ward at the Hightown Mental Health In-patient Unit, Tony McMahon (Aged 83) is reassessed and his detention under the MHA '83 is changed from Section 2 to Section 3 to facilitate his ongoing treatment. Further information since learned about a previous mental health episode is also recorded on the local system as well as the contact details for the social worker assigned to him by the Hightown Social Services - Older Persons team three weeks ago. The resulting changes and additions to the care record are captured by the Nurse on Hatton Ward at the Hightown Mental Health In-patient Unit, using the local provider electronic care record system which then generates an MHA Assessment Update communication which is sent to PSIS.

  1. 5.0          MH&SM-SS Entry

The trigger for an MH&SM-SS Entry communication is the positive decision to enter the patient into the care of a MH&SM-Specialist Service. Care may be delivered by a single professional or a multi- disciplinary team of professionals. The term Entry is used here to denote the beginning of an overall spell of MH&SM-SS care which can only be terminated by an MH&SM-SS Exit communication. An entry is determined by the judgement of a MH&SM-SS healthcare professional that a piece (or pieces) of specialist work is/are required. This may be that a MH&SM specialist service assessment is required following a triage decision regarding the referral / first contact with the patient, or that other more involved or complex care Is required.

The range of information recorded may be as minimal as basic demographics and the triage decision or may include a wide variety of information around the decision to enter the patient as well as care planning and other related MH&SM-SS care information. Detention under the Mental Health Act 1983 may take place at this time if there is a significant concern relating to a risk(s) to or from the patient, and the person is refusing informal admission to hospital. If not detained under the act an inpatient would be classed as an Informal Patient. The decision to enter indicates that care has commenced, whether by a community based service or within an inpatient setting (If this is an entry to an inpatient setting, the communication is generated in addition to any non-MH&SM-SS generic admissions communications).

Patients may also enter MH&SM-SS subject to detention under the MHA '83 by a Crown Court or following an MHA Assessment outside of MH&SM-SS - in these cases a service assessment of need would still take place. The entry into MH&SM-SS would be recorded on PSIS using the MH&SM-SS Entry Communication. 


Scenario 1
Whilst on remand Bill Klein (aged 54) is sent to a local specialist mental health service by Newtown Crown Court following concerns for his mental well being. He is detained by the Court under Section 36 of the Mental Health Act 1983 and transferred from Newtown Prison to the Mid-Town Community Mental Health Trusts - All Souls Hospital. At the point of admission the court papers requiring detention under the Act are presented to the person authorised to receive them at the Mid-Town Mental Health Inpatient Unit. Bill is assessed and admitted to Cavendish, a secure ward in the Unit. The entry to specialist services is recorded by John Hammond, the Staff Nurse receiving Bill onto the ward. At that point John who is also taking on the role of Care Coordinator for Bill completes a Service Assessment and records all the information about him that is available at that time as well as the MHA '83 Legal status as Section 36. The local system then generates an MH&SM-SS Entry communication capturing the information recorded at Entry.

Scenario 2
Jack Beer (aged 32) is referred by his GP to a Substance Misuse service following a recent prolonged period of heavy drinking which he says has been brought on by excessively long working hours. His drinking is beginning to seriously affect his relationship with his wife and his family as well as his work. An assessment by Mark West, a Healthcare professional at the Neston Substance Misuse community clinic, makes it clear that care by the service could help Jack begin to manage his addiction.

There is no legal status under the MHA '83 and care is at this time to be delivered within a community based setting whilst Jack lives at home, under the Models of Care programme. The decision, including all the information captured at the Service Assessment completed by Mark, is recorded on the local system which in-turn generates an MH&SM-SS Entry communication.

Scenario 3
Jayne Vaughan (aged 38) has been depressed following the death of her partner four months ago. She started anti- depressant medication (Seroxat 40mg) three months ago and has only seemed to make minor progress in that time. Her GP, Dr Samuelson is concerned about Jayne's lack of improvement and makes a referral to the North-County Community Mental Health Team (CMHT) for an assessment to see if she requires specialist care. The CMHT discuss the referral at their next team meeting and following a triage process agree that the need for assessment is not urgent; however they decide to offer Jayne an appointment to see Andrea Hitherward (Community Psychiatric Nurse) in two weeks time. This decision is recorded on the local system which in turn generates an MH&SM-SS Entry communication transmission to PSIS.

6.0          MH&SM-SS Update

The trigger for creation of an MH&SM-SS Update communication is any change of or addition to the care record information within a care spell delivered by an MH&SM-SS. This enables any or all of the information items defined for use and relating to MH&SM care in its communications to be updated or added to following an entry to MH&SM-SS.

Scenario 1
Hannah Atkins (aged 47) is currently being cared for within an Adult MH&SM-SS. She is about to leave hospital on Section 17 (MHA '83) leave. This point has been reached following a long period of care during which she has made good progress. It was decided at her last CPA (Care Programme Approach) care review that if her progress continued, she would benefit from a short period of leave in order to begin to reacquaint herself with her family and home situation. The section 17 leave requires approval by the RMO (Registered Medical Officer) Dr Ravinder Singh. Dr Singh and the care team review her case and decide that leave is appropriate at this time. The relevant MHA Paperwork is completed and sent to the MHA Administration office for checking. On receipt of confirmation that the paperwork is in order, Shirley Cross, her Care Coordinator records this amendment to MHA legal status using the local system which triggers the generation of the MH&SM-SS Update communication for transmission to PSIS.

Scenario 2
After a few days in the Uptown Mental Health Inpatient Unit Tony McMahon (Aged 54) is placed on Clozapine medication along with Lithium and Seroxat. At that time a care plan is also drawn up to help him manage his medication; with a view to eventual discharge. At the same time a care plan component to assist Tony to find better accommodation is also drawn up. Tony is involved in drawing up the care plans and at this time fully agrees to them. His Care Coordinator updates his record on the local system. The Medication information is recorded onto PSIS and the Care Plans are recorded using an MH&SM Update Communication which is generated at the same time by the Local System

7.0          MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary

The trigger for the MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary communication is the generation by the Care Coordinator / Lead Professional of summary information from the care review that has just taken place. It provides a review of care and outcomes, and the decisions taken for future activities at the point of the review. As such these reviews should be retrievable with all the information contained within them as it was at the time of entry. They also provide the most recent fixed- point from which local systems can construct a recent care summary (using the most recent MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary and any subsequent MH&SM-SS Update communications stored on PSIS to collate an up to date summary).

The person completing and authorising this summary will be designated as either a Care Coordinator or Lead Professional (the actual title may change from service to service). The professional role of this person may vary depending on each setting and situation and could include: Consultant Psychiatrist, Nurse, Occupational Therapist, Social Worker, Care Worker, etc. If they are the Care Coordinator / Lead Professional and do not have access to a PSIS compliant system then a proxy who is SDS registered can act for them and maintain the patients healthcare record (It is possible for the Care Coordinator / Lead Professional role to be undertaken by professionals working outside of the NHS environment e.g. in voluntary or independent provider agencies).

The key care processes that may generate summaries are: CPA (Care Programme Approach - MH Adults, Older Persons and MH Forensic services as well as within some CAMHS services), MOC (Models of Care - Substance Misuse / Addictions services) or an Unspecified Care Review Summary for anyone not being cared for within a formalised care process but for whom a published care review is required. The frequency of reviews is variable depending on clinical need and policy. For example, CPA has two levels - Standard and Enhanced with reviews often having a local standard of a maximum 12 and 6 monthly intervals respectively. However if the clinical presentation is complex then reviews may take place much more frequently on a planned or unplanned basis.  Each review process has its own set of guidelines and requirements for what should be included or excluded and may include some or all of the communication information items identified for the MH&SM Domain.


Scenario1
After six months in the Uptown Mental Health Inpatient Unit, Tony McMahon's (Aged 54) care is due for a CPA Review. Tom Sanderson his Care Coordinator calls a review which Tony and those involved in his care attend. The review looks at Tony's progress to date and makes decisions about his future care. Not long after he came into hospital Tony stated that he no longer wished to be in hospital and that he was going to discharge himself. He was at that time detained under Section 3 of the Mental Health Act 1983 to ensure his treatment continued.

The CPA Review brings together all the known information about Tony at that time including Care Plans, Risk Assessments, MHA Legal Status as well as supporting other information requirements around CPA. Tom, Tony's Care Coordinator, reviews all this information before he updates his record with the outcome of the Review. He completes the review summary on the local system which in-turn generates a MH&SM Care Review Summary - CPA communication to PSIS
Following the conclusion of a CPA Review Winston Graham, the Care Coordinator, compiles a summary of the review. This activity reviews all the current information recorded on the most recent MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary communication (if a previous one does not exist then the system should use the most recent MH&SM-SS Entry communication) as well as anything updated by more recent MH&SM-SS Updates stored on PSIS. The process requires Winston to verify the information content of each data item recalled. Where information has not changed Winston approves each item in turn (he cannot just approve all the information en-bloc) for continuation into the new CPA Summary.

Where information has changed or there is new information this is added to the summary by the Care Coordinator / Lead Professional. Winston completes the Summary three days after the review, but he makes sure the effective date of the information it contains corresponds to that of the review itself.

Completion of the review summary on the local system triggers the generation of a new MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary for transmission to PSIS.

Scenario 2
Jack Beer (aged 32) has been receiving care from Neston Substance Misuse community clinic for several months and Mark West, a Healthcare professional from that service (Jack's Care Coordinator) calls a Models of Care Review. At the review concerns are raised that Jack isn't adhering to the Care Plan set for him to reduce his daily alcohol intake. It is also noted that he was cautioned by the Police for drunk and disorderly behaviour three weeks ago. The care team at review thought that a period of time in the services Inpatient Unit to take Jack through a programme of complete withdrawal from alcohol with aftercare would benefit him significantly. Concerns were also raised at the review from information received from his G.P. Dr Althea Kirk that he seems to be presenting a different story to her about his progress. Dr Kirk was invited to attend the review, but was unfortunately not able to attend. She has asked Mark to keep her up to date with Jack's progress and has offered to cooperate with the Substance Misuse service in Jack's care.  In subsequent discussions with Jack, Mark has gained his agreement  to a new Care Plan which includes the inpatient care spell. A new risk assessment had been completed for Jack in preparation for the Care Review which indicates that his level of self-neglect has significantly increased.

It is now three days after the review and Mark completes the Care Review Summary. When Mark recalls Jack's record from the system, it presents all the current information against each data item. Mark reviews all the current entries and approves each one in turn. He also makes any amendments and additions as a result of the outcome of the review. He enters the new Care Plan; one aspect of which is a request to Dr Althea Kirk (Jack's G.P.) to keep the Substance Misuse service up to date with any concerns she has following any appointments she has with Jack. Mark also adds the new Risk Assessment on Jack's record and makes sure that all the effective dates of activities in the Review reflect the actual date of the review rather than this communication. At the conclusion of the review summary the local system generates an MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary - Models of Care communication for Mark to approve. On approval of this communication the local system sends it to PSIS. At the same time it also generates an MH&SM Notification to Jack's G.P., Dr Kirk, outlining that the new Care Plan contains actions for her and that she should look at Jack's record via PSIS where the MH&SM-SS Care Review Summary - Models of Care communication is held.

Scenario 3
Georgia Harrison (aged 9 years) has been attending appointments with Frances Meadows, a Clinical Psychologist based with the Mid-Town CAMHS (Child and Adolescent Mental Health Service) Unit, for behavioural problems that started following the death of her twin sister Georgina some 18 months ago. Frances is the only professional involved in Georgia's care and has been seeing her for about a year now. It is service policy to review all patients at the end of the first year of care and Frances arranges the review with other members of the CAMHS Team. Despite some earlier difficulties around the anniversary of the death of Georgia's sister that required a longer than normal engagement with her, Frances feels that she is nearing the end of her input to Georgina's care . The review takes the form of one agreed locally by the Mid-Town CAMH Service.

Frances has completed a risk assessment prior to the review for Georgia which indicates a marked decrease in all areas of risk. Frances decides to continue the care on a gradually reducing basis over the next three months; at which time it is to conclude. The review records a recommendation to Georgia's G.P. that if she shows any adverse behaviour following exit (discharge) from the service within the next year, that she should be referred back to the CAMH Service.

At the conclusion of the review, Frances records the review on the local system as Georgia's Specialist Professional. She reviews all the information on the record, approving that which is unchanged and current. At the same time she enters a summarised outcome of the review on the record as well as the updated Risk Assessment. When she finishes entering the information onto the local system it generates an MH&SM Care Review Summary - Unspecified communication for her to approve. She realises she has missed off the recommendation to the G.P, from the review and so Frances does not approve the communication. She adds in the items she had forgotten and concludes the review. The system then re-generates a new MH&SM Care Review Summary - Unspecified which it renders to her for approval. This time on approval it is transmitted to PSIS.

8.0          MH&SM-SS Exit

The trigger for creation of an MH&SM-SS Exit communication is the end of a spell of care by an MH&SM-SS. Entry at its earliest point may only determine that an assessment by specialist services is required following a triage decision. At this point, following assessment but without any treatment a patient may be exited from the service with no further MH&SM Specialist Service care. The other use of this communication is where a spell of care within MH&SM Specialist Services takes place and for which care is continued beyond the Service Assessment; this is described below.

Following a review of care, the Care Coordinator / Lead Professional will have recorded in the patient's record the decision to end care by the MH&SM-SS using an MH&SM Care Review Summary communication. The decision to end care and the actual exit may or may not take place at the same time and therefore this communication is only generated at the actual point of discharge / exit from Specialist Services. The transmission of the MH&SM-SS Exit communication formally terminates the spell of care by MH&SM-Specialist Services. It should be noted that this communication should only be generated when all of the conditions regarding exit have been achieved (e.g. that there are no active sections of the MHA still applicable - except MHA Section 117, and that no other activities which may restrict exit, e.g. Non-MHA court or other legal/statutory restrictions, apply). This communication takes place independently of any non-MH&SM-SS generic discharge communications that may be generated at this time; however it should always accompany any generic discharge communications sent to the patient's GP.

NB Any further MH&SM-SS activity after this communication has been generated will require a new MH&SM-SS Entry communication denoting the commencement of a new spell of care.

Scenario 1
Tony McMahon (Aged 54) was discharged from Uptown Mental Health Inpatient Unit two years ago after making excellent progress. Tony was also discharged from Section 3 of the Mental Health Act 1983 at that time following an MHA Tribunal which gave him a discharge from the Section conditional on his maintaining his adherence to his medication care plan. This has continued to be the case and the Uptown Community Mental Health Team is now ready to end the care spell within MH&SM Specialist Services. At his final CPA Review held a week ago the decision was taken to exit him from MH&SM Specialist Services into the care of his G.P. with the provision that if he shows any sign of relapse the Uptown Community Mental Health Trust will be available to support him. On the day of Tony's exit from care by the MH&SM Specialist Service Andrew Jones his current Care Coordinator completes an Exit summary on the local system. This includes information about the care he has received. It also gives details of all the support activities that will be continuing as well as recommendations for monitoring, aftercare and support. When he has finished recording the Exit information Andrew approves the communication and the system generates an MH&SM Exit communication for publication to PSIS.

Scenario
Following a recent care review a decision was taken that Neil Johnson's (age 26) care within MH&SM-SS should be ended. This decision was taken on the 20th May; however it was decided that he could not be immediately discharged until accommodation could be secured for him.
