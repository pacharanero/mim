Business Information Model Technical The Business Information Model is a basic overview of the information concepts and their relationships with the business area or domain, this is represented diagrammatically and specifically identifies: 1. Identification of the business area(s) and system(s) with which the domain is concerned 2. Identification of classes (information concepts) that are shared with other domains 3. Identification of classes (information concepts) that are the source and destination of communications between business area(s) and between system(s) It is not intended to be a definitive document, so will not capture the entire information requirement and their associated flows; however it is intended that the content should be understandable and recognisable to business users.

Business Process Model Technical The Business Process Model shows one, many or all possible configurations of the Detailed Use Cases within the domain. The activities within the Business Process Models are the Detailed Use Cases from within the Use Case Palette, connected by process logic. Where such logic between Detailed Use Cases exists, this should also be represented within the Detailed Use Case Model Set (as pre/post conditions and triggers). However the BPM may not represent the definitive statement of process within the (part of the) domain, since non-Detailed Use Case activities are excluded from the BPM scope.

Care Pathway Business a structured plan of care, for a particular problem or issue, that draws upon good practice and past experience and provides a template for a patient’s care plan. The Care Pathway may involve treatments and interventions by more that one health care service
Care Plan Business The plan that is to be followed in caring for a particular patient. This will normally be based on one or more Care Pathways. If the patient has only one problem, and there are no exceptional circumstances, it may exactly follow a single Care Pathway; but it may be tailored to individual needs. Where a patient has more than one problem, then the Patient’s Care Plan will typically be the combination of two or more Care Pathways. Even where many Care Pathways are incorporated, there can be only one care plan for the patient. (The patient cannot receive incompatible concurrent treatments, or be in two places at once.)
