Scenario 1 (Contact Assess: GP, Overview Assess: Social Worker) 

Rachel Walters, a lady aged 46, has had mobility difficulties since a car accident several months ago.  She visits her GP Dr Alan Norman at Church Lane Surgery for a medication review, but is also asked about how she is coping.  Rachel tells him that she is having increasing difficulties with washing, bathing and other personal care.  Dr Norman completes a contact assessment which he sends to Hebburn Social Services to request an assessment.  

[see IE Scenario 1 - Contact Assessment]

The contact assessment is received by the single point of contact team at Hebburn Social services. They check their records and see that Ms Walters is not known to social services.   A social worker Leigh Timpson arranges an appointment with Ms Walters. They complete relevant sections of an overview assessment. Ms Walters explains that she receives some support from her son, but realises that in order to remain living independently in her home as she wishes, she will require some additional support services for personal care.
Her decreased mobility also means she is unable to get out as much as she would like, and is at risk of social isolation.

[see IE Scenario 1 - Overview Assessment]

Leigh produces a care plan containing only social care support, which Mrs Walters is happy with. A copy is given to Mrs Walters, and a copy is also sent to Dr Norman.
