Scenario 2 (Contact: Hospital, Overview: Social Worker, Specialist Assessments: OT & Physio)

Barry Jones is a 37 year old man who had a stroke several months ago and is being cared for at home by his wife, Audrey. He was recently admitted to Ward 5, Hebburn Hospital from the emergency department, following a fall when getting out of the shower and with a body rash caused by antibiotics prescribed for a chest infection by his GP. Whilst on the ward, Leticia Gulliver, the ward sister, completes a contact assessment for Mr Jones and refers him to Nick Cotterell-Chapman, who is the ward physiotherapist, for a mobility assessment and to Hebburn social services as she feels carer support may be needed when Mr Jones returns home.

[see IE Scenario 1 - Contact Assessment]

The ward physiotherapist assesses him on the ward, using a locally developed mobility assessment and records a summary of the outcome of the assessment and his recommendations, which is held in Mr Jones’ care record so that it is available to the care team and is sent to the community physiotherapist.

[see IE Scenario 2 - Specialist Assessment Outcome 1]

As part of the discharge planning process, Sharon Adamson, who is a community social worker, carries out an overview assessment with Mr Jones and identifies a heavy dependency on his wife who is also his carer.  She offers a carer’s assessment to his wife.  Mr Jones talks about the difficulties he is having with getting round his home. He has problems carrying out his personal care and is feeling frustrated as he wants to get back into work.   She refers him to the occupational therapy team at Hebburn PCT.

[see IE Scenario 2 - Overview Assessment]

Sharon Adamson carries out a carer’s assessment for Mrs Jones, which is recorded in her record, but no support is required at the present time.

The occupational therapy team administrator, Claire Osmund, at Hebburn PCT receives the overview assessment and Mr Jones’ case is allocated to Claudette Pendry-King, Senior occupational therapist.  Claudette visits Mr Jones at home and carries out a functional assessment of Mr Jones. The assessment is done on paper in Mr Jones’ home, and a copy is left with him. The assessment is written up in the office by Claire Osmund, who sends the summary of the outcomes to Sharon Adamson, with a copy to Dr Natalie Atkinson, his GP at Park View Surgery. 

[see IE Scenario 2 - Specialist Assessment Outcome 2]

Sharon Adamson acts as care co-ordinator and, from the specialist assessments and her own overview assessment, pulls together an integrated care and support plan, including all the health and social care support that will be provided for Mr Jones.
