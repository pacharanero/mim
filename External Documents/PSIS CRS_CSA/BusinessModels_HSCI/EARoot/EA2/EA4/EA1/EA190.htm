Continuing Care Assessment is carried out by practitioners (generally Health Care, but Social care may contribute) to determine whether a persons needs are primarily related to their health, and whether they are eligible for continuing care services funded by the NHS.

Eligibility criteria is set by the commissioning PCT. Some work is ongoing within the DH to standardise criteria.

Information collected as part of Continuing Care Assessment may also be used to determine eligibility for FACS, if individual is not eligible for Continuing Care.
