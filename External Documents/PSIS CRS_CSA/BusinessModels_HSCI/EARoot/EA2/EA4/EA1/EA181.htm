The overall process for identifying and recording the health and social care risks and needs of an individual and evaluating their impact on daily living and quality of life, so that appropriate action can be planned.

In general, assessment goes though stages;
- an initial / contact/screening assessment;
- an in-depth/ overview/ holistic assessment;
- re-assessment or review.

Assessment also includes concepts of:
(1) an assessment of eligibility for services, based on level of needs identified. For adult social care an eligibility framework - Fair Access to Care Services (FACS) has been developed.
(2) an assessment of the mental capacity of the person.
(3) Investigation. Clinical Assessments may include test/ investigations/ examinations and observations to formulate a clinical diagnosis.

Current practice usually involves a practitioner carrying out an assessment with an individual, however there is significant movement towards enabling self-assessment of social care needs.
