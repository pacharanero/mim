The existence of the abuse of vulnerable adults by relatives, friends, neighbours, and strangers as well as by those employed to protect them has long been acknowledged. There is now an increased awareness of the level and extent of adult abuse and the need for a pro-active response. The Department of Health and Home Office issued No Secrets Guidance on developing Multi-Agency Policies and Procedures to protect vulnerable adults from abuse in March 2000.

Some authorities keep a register of vulnerable adults (often using system functionality that supports child protection registers). Lots of variation in working practices around the country.

As part of the implementation of the Care Standards Act (2000), the Department of Health introduced the Protection of Vulnerable Adults (POVA) list of offenders on 26 July 2004, as a complement to the requirement for the development of local multi-agency adult protection policies and procedures, set out in No Secrets (DH, 2000). POVA list is list of offenders held by Central Government of people who have committed offences or been referred by employer following dismissal. Link with Criminal Records Bureau.

The POVA list is being run by the Department for Education and Skills (DfES) on behalf of the Department of Health (DH, 2004a). A similar list exists in relation to children, the POCA (Protection of Children Act, 1999) list.
