In the case of children, a guardian might also be considered to be the carer of the child. For adults carer's do not necessarily have any legal responsibility for the individual they care for. Note: Children may also be considered carer's.

- Guardian:
Legal responsibility for the individual who is in receipt of care or services.

- Carer:
Defined as providing: " Regular and Substantive care" to the individual who is in receipt of care or services.

CSSRs have some statutory responsibilities towards carer's, such as offering carer's assessments, either individually, or jointly with the person who is in receipt of care or services.
