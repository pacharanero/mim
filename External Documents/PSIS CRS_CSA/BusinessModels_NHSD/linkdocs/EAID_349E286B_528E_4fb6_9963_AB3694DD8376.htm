  1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide the necessary background/information required to determine the scope of the Business Process Model and associated design-related work products that are to be created for the multi agent care for NHS Direct. It will define the boundaries that need to be analysed to allow the interactions between NHS Direct and the CfH programmes to be identified.

That information includes:

  • The version of the NHS Direct CAS Software to which the modelling will be based;

  • Extent of the BPM(s) and related deliverables to be produced by definition of the start and end points for each model;

  • Identification of other processes which interact at the model(s) boundaries;

  • Assumptions to be made in producing the deliverables; and

  • Control arrangements in terms of ownership and custodianship.

  1. Introduction

    Business process definitions are required by many of the component parts of CfH and its wider stakeholders. In order to reduce the possibility of inconsistencies and to maximise the value of the resulting process definitions, there needs to be a consistent approach to reconciling the requirements of the different users within a BPM.
    The BPM will conform to the standard Business Process Modelling guidelines as stated in Process Modelling User Guide

    1. Domain/ Business Area Description

      NHS Direct has developed into a high quality, health advice and information service operating in England and Wales since its inception in March 1998.

      NHS Direct, now part of the mainstream NHS, is set to grow and improve its services.  This includes the 24- hour telephone service, the website and in time digital TV access to health advice, information and support to people across the county.

      The service aims to help people make choices about their own healthcare and be the patient's portal to Choose and Book and where necessary guide them to the most appropriate level of care to meet their needs, particularly out-of-hours.

      Using its national infrastructure and resources NHS Direct is also uniquely positioned to support the NHS on a range of modernisation issues outlined in the NHS Plan and other Department of Health strategies, whilst working with local health networks to support locally developed services11 Department of Health, NHS Direct Commissioning Framework, 2004.

    2. Domain / Model Requirements

For this release of the BPM and other design artefacts it shall be restricted to NHS Direct telephone contact only. Other forms of use of NHS Direct (e.g. online, digital TV) shall be considered in future releases as they become available. With this in mind the following activities will be covered:

  • Call handling (conventional and streaming);

  • Triage;

  • Provision of health information advice;

  • Advice given by Nurse Advisor; and

  • Forwarding to out-of-hours, emergency services and GP daytime services.


There maybe a requirement for one or more models to cover the above.

  1. Phasing Statement

    1. Basis for Modelling

      This work covered by this scoping document utilises the Provision of Care Discharge domain/sub-set message specifications as detailed in the MiM version 4.0.
      Additionally, the modelling will be based on v10 of the NHS Direct CAS software, as to a large extent this dictates the process that is followed within NHS Direct centres. However, this shall not be the sole basis of the modelling as observation of NHS Direct local procedure shall also contribute to the modelling activity.

    2. Target for Models

      The models specified in this document will be utilised in MiM 5.0. It is highly unlikely that the messages defined for NHSD in MiM 5.0 will be implemented in 2007, despite MiM 5.0 being targeted for this release. Therefore NHSD messages shall appear as draft and for further discussion in MiM 5.0.

  2. Related Work Products

In addition to the scoping document the following work products to be created. This will complete the design of NHSD.

  • Business Process Models (both complex and simple, the latter for the purpose of discussion with NHSD);

  • Detailed data content;

  • Expected system functionality;

  • Clinical scenarios;

  • Message Implementation Manual (MiM) which includes

    • Implementation guidance;

    • Models;

    • Schemas;

    • Example messages.

  • Scoping Requirements

    1. Overview of Process

      The scope of this work will be focused towards the high level business functions within NHS Direct, this covers call handling, call streaming (a system where a call handler identify and directly refer patients clearly needing face to face consultation to the most appropriate service provider), triage and advice by a nurse and health information advice. The model will cover how calls are handled and where they are escalated (including those in an emergency).  The model will include the processes for redirecting queries through to other NHS services. A separate set of high level models shall be developed that demonstrate the basic flow of the business functions.

    2. Start/End Points of the BPM

      The BPM is to commence from a call to the NHS Direct call handler within the domain / business area.

      The BPM is to end at disposition of the caller from NHS Direct. (NB during work on the BPM it may be found that there is more than one End Point).

    3. Out of Scope Activities

      The work focuses primarily on those elements of the business process where NHS Direct is using CfH Services or data by connecting to CfH systems. It does not attempt to cover in any detail the internal processes of NHS Direct or the operation of NHS Direct IT systems (for example OOH referrals within NHSD).

    4. Exception Conditions to be Included/Excluded from the BPM

      An exception condition is deemed to be when the process is interrupted. For example non receipt of an acknowledgement or any other situation where checks are made and the checks fail.
      In general, the exception conditions to be explicitly modelled are of the 'flow failure' type - i.e. where a flow in the model can fail (such as the non-receipt of an acknowledgement message) NB. This applies only where the prospect of the failure is realistic specific cases of this type of failure are identified in Section 5.4.1 below.

      1. Explicit Exception Conditions

    • The following exception conditions are to be included within the process model:

    • Failure of call back;

    • Failure of patients to call ambulance.

        1. Assumed Exception Conditions

    The following exception conditions can be excluded from the process model:

    • Security failure;

    • Infrastructure failure.

    1. Backward Compatibility

      Any new system which is integrated to a National service (e.g. PSIS) will need to use the latest version of the message. If the message changes, then any existing system will need to update its message to comply with any future message schema, and complete compliance testing to achieve accreditation. New systems sending information to National services will be expected to support the most recent version of the message.  

      Existing systems will be expected to support messages which are current or no more than 1 major version behind the current version. Systems receiving information from National services will be expected to support the most recent version, and the previous version of the message. The National services will support the most recent version, and the previous version of the message. All systems sending or receiving information to National service will be expected to migrate to the new version over a period of time; typically we would expect no more than two major versions to be supported at any point in time.

    2. Relationships to Other Processes

      The following boundary processes are referenced or interacted with from within the scope of this model which includes: GP Surgeries; Out Of Hours; Emergency Department and the Ambulance Services.
      The interaction with social services and emergency services such as police and fire will be included if deemed relevant (through discussions with NHS Direct).

    3. Assumptions

      The following assumptions are to be made in constructing this model:

      1. User Assumptions

    User (business) assumptions to be covered are as follows:

    • The caller on the telephone is who he/she says they are - This is a major assumption, and as yet there is no defined policy for this. IG/IQAP guidance on remote patient communication is required before this can be safely assumed.

    • The user is able to identify key triggers that would escalate the caller's query;

    • Contact details are recorded;

    • English may not be the first language to answer queries;

    • The user is fully trained to use the system and telephone;

    • The user is fully trained in procedures and relevant contact details to handle and escalate queries; and

    • The user is aware of confidentiality issues;

    • Consent will be sought from the patient to share the care record sent to PSIS as a result of an NHS Direct encounter;

    • Consent can be overridden by NHS Direct if it is in the interest of public safety and/or the subject of care is unable to give consent (e.g. the subject is a child or is mentally incapable of providing consent);

    • There will be an attempt to retrieve the PDS record for the subject of care.

      1. System Assumptions

    System assumptions to be covered are as follows:

    • The suppliers are willing/able to make the changes to their software;

    • The necessary infrastructure for security and hardware is in place; and

    • Telephone lines and equipment are in working order.

      1. Message Assumptions

  • It is assumed that PSIS, PDS, RBAC and LR messages will be used (the information will be carried via PoC messages);

  • No care event will be sent to PSIS where only health information is provided;

  • The message will be sent at the end of the NHS Direct assessment of the subject of care;

  • Messaging to PSIS to create a care event relating to a specific NHSD call will only occur if the identity of the subject of care can be confirmed via the PDS record;

  • At this time it is assumed that a simplified PoC message will be used as the basis of NHS Direct messaging. Therefore no specific new messages for NHSD will be required.
