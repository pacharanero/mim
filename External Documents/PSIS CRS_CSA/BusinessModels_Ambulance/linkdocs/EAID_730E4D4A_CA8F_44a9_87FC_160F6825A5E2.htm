Ambulance Service ERG Messaging Sub Group

  1. Scenario: Barry Jones (Adult with Myocardial Infarction)


Barry Jones, a 46 year old man, is at home when he experiences crushing central chest pain and looses consciousness. On hearing his fall, his wife Cathy Jones rushes into the room and dials 999. Cathy is transferred by the operator to the Yorkshire Ambulance Service (YAS) Control Centre where her call is answered immediately.

The control centre operative begins a 'call' on her system (logged at 20:36:02 hrs). She asks Cathy a series of questions designed to identify where the caller is, where the person she is calling about is, what is wrong with him and what priority the call should be handled in.

Using the YAS system, she dispatches the quickest 'Call Responder' type and sends them the details of the call. The call responder is on the way to the location ('despatched') within 10 seconds (at 20:36:13 hrs). In this case, the system has chosen an emergency paramedic ambulance 'YAS 105' as they are less than 1 mile away from the caller's location.

The Ambulance arrives at Barry's house and the crew inform control that they are on scene.. This logs them as arriving at 20:38:43 hrs. The crew start to check Barry's vital signs whilst asking his wife if she knows anything about what caused this event.

They learn that during his regular dialysis he was noted to have high blood pressure and was due to return to have his blood pressure checked in a few days time.
All the information gathered is entered onto the Mobile Data Terminal (MDT) in the appropriate sections of the screen. They transfer him to the ambulance, and perform an ECG which confirms a Myocardial Infarction. They administer Thrombolysis treatment by infusion. Barry Jones recovers consciousness and is able to tell them about his chest pain.

The crew enter the destination details into the MDT - this informs the Emergency Department that they are on the way in.

The nearest hospital is Northern General, which is approximately 5 miles away. The ambulance arrives there 8 minutes later. They transfer Barry Jones directly to CCU

The crew book him into the CCU and hand over to the nursing staff by telling them what they had been called for, what they had found, what they had observed and treated.

The crew then declare their availability on the MDT, logging the end of the call at 21:10:54 hrs. This prompts the Local Ambulance System to produce a summary Ambulance Service Patient Report for the event that is then sent to PSIS and Barry Jones's GP.
