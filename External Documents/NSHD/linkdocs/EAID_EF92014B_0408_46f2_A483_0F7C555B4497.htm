Introduction to Analysis

This is the formal output of the NHSD domain. There are a large number of documents and models (artefacts) and their purpose is described below.

Scope

The Analysis Scope and Objective Definition document is prepared during the initiation of a project and describes what the analysis is trying to achieve within the domain. It states the business aims of the project, including setting the business context of the work, and from that derives a detailed scope for the analysis work as well as identifying what analysis artefacts are required.

Business Flows

The Business Process Model shows one, many or all possible configurations of the business processes concerned with NHSD .
Within the domain there are multiple discrete business processes and the relationships between those discrete business processes are represented as discontinuous flows. To guide the reader there are example configurations which illustrate some, but not all, of the ways in which the discrete business process may be linked.
During the development of the business process model there were many amendments and refinements during internal and external review and the audit trail is contained within the Model Change History.

The business process model may be a large and complex artefact set and the key business processes have been represented on a Detailed Use Case Diagram which aims to specify the business functionality required within the domain and provide the audience with an organised view of the requirements across the domain from a user's perspective

Interactions and sequences

The related interactions between sender and receiver (Business Application Roles) are represented in the Interaction Units which consists of sequence diagrams showing the sequencing of related business interactions which together complete a transaction.

Data content

The business information content of NHSD and associated interaction messages have been developed and refined using a series of structured data models (or “class models”). These are the Business Information Model, Information Analysis Model and Communication Descriptions.

Examples

A real-life example of the process and surrounding circumstance is provided for ease of understanding and to give context to the analysis. This is done in the form of written Scenarios with supporting data/information modelled as Information Examples.

Functionality

There are many aspects of NHSD which are not described by the process or data models but rely on the clinical systems possessing functionality which is outside of current requirements, and these are contained within the Expected Functionality.

Clinical Safety

Within any electronic interchange there is a potential for errors and failure points, if these are known they can be mitigated against to reduce the likelihood or reduce their impact. The known clinical risks are contained within the Clinical Risks Register.

Implementation

When the electronic interchange of NHSD is implemented there are a number of factors which must be taken into account, these are detailed within the Implementation Register.
